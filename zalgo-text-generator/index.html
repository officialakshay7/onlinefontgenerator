What Is a Zalgo Text Generator?

Zalgo text takes the combining diacritical mark technique used by regular glitch text to its extreme conclusion. Each character receives dozens of randomly-chosen combining marks stacked above, through, and below it — creating the visual impression of text that is overflowing, corrupted, or possessed. The term "Zalgo" comes from a creepypasta internet meme originating around 2004.

The marks are drawn from the full Unicode Combining Diacritical Marks block and surrounding ranges, including combining characters intended for phonetics, historical scripts, and ancient languages — repurposed here for maximum visual chaos.

Expert Tips for Zalgo & Cursed Text

Get better results with these pro techniques.

1

Less is more — even with Zalgo

Use Zalgo on one key word (typically your username or a focal term) surrounded by clean text. Full Zalgo paragraphs are completely unreadable.

2

Use it for dramatic reveals

Post plain text first, then reply or comment with the Zalgo version as a 'corruption' callback — it's a popular storytelling technique in horror communities.

3

Pair with Gothic text

The combination of Gothic Fraktur + Zalgo diacritics is the canonical internet horror aesthetic. The two styles were essentially designed for each other.

4

Understand platform limits

Very heavy Zalgo text can occasionally cause rendering issues in some apps due to the sheer number of combining characters. Test before use in critical contexts.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the Zalgo Text Generator.

Is Zalgo text safe to use everywhere?
Zalgo text uses standard Unicode combining characters and is technically safe. However, extremely heavy zalgo strings can cause display issues or excessive line height in some apps. Mild and medium intensities are the safest bet.
Does Zalgo text work on Discord?
Yes. Discord renders Zalgo text correctly in messages, usernames, and server names. It's extremely popular in horror-themed Discord communities.
Why is it called 'Zalgo'?
The name comes from a 4chan and Something Awful-era creepypasta featuring a demonic entity called Zalgo. The corrupted text style became associated with its disturbing, reality-breaking imagery.
