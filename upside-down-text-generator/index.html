What Is a Upside Down Text Generator?

Upside down text generators use Unicode characters from various phonetics and IPA blocks that happen to look like common letters rotated 180 degrees. The text is also reversed so that it reads correctly when the reader tilts their screen — or reads left-to-right when the text is displayed upside-down. For example, 'a' becomes 'ɐ', 'e' becomes 'ǝ', and 'h' becomes 'ɥ'.

The technique relies on characters originally designed for the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA) and other linguistic notation systems. Most of the 26 letters have a usable upside-down equivalent, though some (like x and o) look identical when flipped.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the Upside Down Text Generator.

How does upside down text work?
Each letter is replaced with a Unicode character that resembles the original letter rotated 180 degrees. The entire string is also reversed so that it reads left-to-right when the device (or the reader) is turned upside down.
Does upside down text work on all platforms?
Most upside down characters are from the IPA and Latin Extended blocks and are well-supported. A few unusual characters may not render on very old devices, but common letters all have strong support.
Can people actually read upside down text?
Yes, with a little effort. Most readers immediately understand the trick and tilt their head or screen to read it — which is part of the engagement appeal.
Is the text truly upside down?
The individual characters are rotated equivalents, and the string is reversed. It's a very convincing approximation but not a perfect geometric rotation — some letters (like 's') look the same upside down and may appear as 's' rather than a flipped version.
