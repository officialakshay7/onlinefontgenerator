What Is a Glitch Text Generator?

Glitch text generators add Unicode combining diacritical marks to regular characters — tiny accent marks, overlines, underlines, and strikethroughs that stack on top of and below each letter. The result is corrupted, glitched-looking text that still functions as plain text and can be copied and pasted anywhere.

Three intensity levels are available: Mild glitch for a subtle distortion effect, Medium for a heavily corrupted look, and Heavy for maximum chaos. Each uses different combinations of combining marks drawn from the Unicode Combining Diacritical Marks block (U+0300–U+036F).

Expert Tips for Glitch & Distortion Text

Get better results with these pro techniques.

1

Use mild glitch for usernames

The mild intensity is still readable and unique. Heavy glitch becomes unreadable very quickly — save it for artistic or decorative purposes only.

2

One glitched word is enough

Glitching your entire bio defeats the purpose. Apply glitch to one standout word — your name or brand — and let the rest be plain. The contrast is the effect.

3

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the Glitch Text Generator.

How does glitch text actually work?
Glitch text uses Unicode combining characters — small diacritical marks (like accents, tildes, and dots) that attach above and below base characters. Stacking multiple combining marks creates the corrupted, glitched visual effect.
Is glitch text readable?
Mild glitch is still very readable. Medium and heavy glitch are intentionally difficult to read — this is the aesthetic. Use intensity appropriate to your context.
Does glitch text work on all platforms?
Combining diacritical marks are part of the core Unicode standard and render on virtually all modern platforms. Some older apps may strip combining marks or render them incorrectly.
Can I undo the glitch?
Yes. The glitch characters only exist in the copied text. The original text in our generator is unchanged — just clear and retype to start fresh.
