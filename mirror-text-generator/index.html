What Is a Mirror Text Generator?

Mirror text generators reverse the order of characters in a string and replace each letter with its horizontally mirrored Unicode equivalent. The result reads right-to-left and looks like text reflected in a mirror. Unlike simple text reversal, true mirror text uses modified letterforms — 'b' becomes 'd', 'p' becomes 'q', 'R' becomes 'Я' — to complete the mirror illusion.

The mirrored letterforms are drawn from multiple Unicode blocks including Latin Extended, Cyrillic, and various phonetic and mathematical character sets that happen to contain the reversed forms of common Latin letters.

Expert Tips for Mirror & Reversed Text

Get better results with these pro techniques.

1

Works best for short words

Mirror text is most convincing for 1–3 word phrases. Longer strings lose the mirror illusion as letter-by-letter replacement accumulates small inaccuracies.

2

Use the actual Cyrillic Я

The reversed capital R (Я) from Cyrillic is so well-known that using it in isolation (e.g. "ЯeverseD") is a legible stylistic choice even without full mirror text.

3

Combine with upside down

Using mirror text on alternate lines with upside-down text creates a full 'scrambled reality' effect popular in dark or surrealist aesthetic accounts.

4

Test symmetrical words

Words like 'noon', 'level', and 'radar' are palindromes — test them in the mirror generator to see if they produce interesting symmetrical outputs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the Mirror Text Generator.

Is mirror text the same as reversed text?
No. Reversed text ('olleh') simply reverses character order. Mirror text also replaces each character with its mirrored letterform equivalent, so individual letters look reflected as well as the overall string reading right-to-left.
Can you read mirror text without a mirror?
Yes, with practice — or by holding your phone up to a real mirror. The brain quickly learns to decode simple mirror text, especially for short words.
Does mirror text work on Instagram?
Yes. The mirrored Unicode characters are standard glyphs and render correctly on all modern platforms including Instagram, Twitter, and Discord.
Why do some letters look the same mirrored?
Letters like 'A', 'H', 'I', 'M', 'O', 'T', 'U', 'V', 'W', 'X', 'Y' are horizontally symmetrical. They look identical in mirror text, which is mathematically correct.
